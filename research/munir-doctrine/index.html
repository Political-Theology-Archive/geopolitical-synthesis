Abstract

Field Marshal Syed Asim Munir Ahmed Shah's appointment as COAS (November 2022) and Pakistan's first Chief of Defence Forces (December 2025) represents the completion of a civilizational arc: Zia ul-Haq's Deobandization (Ba'alist Capture phase), Musharraf's hikmat phase, and Munir's theological institutionalization of the Iqbalian-Sufi vision. Three defining developments: the TTP Fitna al-Khawarij designation (July 26, 2024) — the first Pakistani state intervention in intra-Islamic theological debate using a category from Imam Ali (A.S.)'s struggle at Nahrawan; Riyasat-e-Tayyaba translating Iqbal's khudi into state doctrine; and Pakistan as US-Iran mediator in 2025-2026.

I. The Civilizational Arc — Three Phases

WP-12 reads the arc from Zia to Munir as a single civilizational event in three phases, not as three separate periods of Pakistani political history:

Phase I — Zia ul-Haq 1977-1988  ·  Ba'alist Capture Phase

Zia ul-Haq's Islamization program represents the successful Ba'alist Capture of Pakistan's zahir — the institutional and legal apparatus of the state — by the Deobandi-Wahhabi-Saudi complex documented in WP-06, WP-16, and WP-39. The Deobandization of the Army's chaplaincy and officer culture; the introduction of Sharia courts with Deobandi jurisprudential dominance; the creation of the Afghan mujahideen jihad infrastructure funded by Saudi petrodollars and directed by the CIA. Pakistan's batin (the Iqbalian-Sufi synthesis) was systematically suppressed while its zahir was reconstructed around the Deobandi template.

Phase II — Musharraf 1999-2008  ·  Hikmat Phase  ·  WP-11

Musharraf's post-2001 conduct represents hikmat — strategic wisdom operating under structural constraint — not the duplicity that Western analysis attributed to him. WP-11 documents the systematic extraction of $23 billion in economic relief while dismantling Deobandi extremist infrastructure through institutional bans, madrassa reform legislation, and the Lal Masjid operation (July 2007). Musharraf could not frontally confront the Deobandi infrastructure built over three decades — but he could contain, reduce, and begin reversing it. The hikmat phase is the transition between capture and restoration.

Phase III — Asim Munir 2022-present  ·  Theological Completion Phase ← WP-12

Munir's appointment as COAS on November 27, 2022 inaugurates the phase of explicit theological institutionalization. Where Zia Deobandized the Pakistani state's batin vocabulary and Musharraf conducted the hikmat phase of tactical containment, Munir explicitly re-Iqbalianizes Pakistani state theology — reinstituting the batin tradition that Ba'alist Capture had suppressed. The three defining developments analyzed in WP-12 mark the precise theological content of this phase.

II. Three Defining Developments of the Munir Doctrine
Development I — Fitna al-Khawarij Designation  ·  July 26, 2024

On July 26, 2024, the Pakistan Army formally designated the Tehrik-e-Taliban Pakistan (TTP) as Fitna al-Khawarij — drawing on the Imamic theological category of the Khawarij (the "seceders" who left Imam Ali (A.S.)'s camp at Nahrawan in 37 AH / 657 CE). This is the first time in Pakistan's history that the state has intervened in an intra-Islamic theological debate using a specific jurisprudential category derived from the Imami tradition.

The Nahrawan parallel: The historical Khawarij displayed maximum zahir piety (the "reciters of the Quran" whose foreheads were calloused from prostration) while rejecting the walayah declaration of Ghadir and demanding that Imam Ali (A.S.) submit to their interpretation of "governance belongs to God alone" (la hukm illa lillah). Imam Ali (A.S.)'s response at Nahrawan was military — he defeated them in battle. The TTP designation applies the same category: the TTP displays maximum zahir Islamic performance (the beard, the veil, the Sharia courts) while attacking the Islamic state that represents the legitimate authority.

The SCRA significance: by using the Khawarij category, Munir has made a theological claim that the TTP's violence is not jihad (which would give it Islamic legitimacy) but fitna (sedition against legitimate authority) — the same category Imam Ali (A.S.) used at Nahrawan. This is the Furqan criterion (WP-24) deployed at state level.

Development II — Riyasat-e-Tayyaba  ·  The Iqbalian State Doctrine

Riyasat-e-Tayyaba ("the Pure State" — from Quran 14:24's metaphor of the pure tree) is the state doctrine developed under Munir's tenure, translating Iqbal's khudi (self-realization through divine proximity) into administrative language. Where Deobandi state doctrine emphasized zahir compliance (enforcement of dress codes, prayer times, Sharia courts), Riyasat-e-Tayyaba emphasizes the inward transformation of the citizen — the batin development that produces authentic Islamic character rather than coerced zahir conformity. This is the institutionalization of Iqbal's distinction between zahir Islam (the shell) and batin Islam (the living content).

Development III — Pakistan as US-Iran Mediator  ·  2025-2026

Pakistan's emergence as the primary back-channel between the United States and Iran in 2025-2026 represents the geopolitical expression of the Munir Doctrine: a Pakistan Army that has re-established its Iqbalian-Sufi civilizational identity is uniquely positioned to mediate between the Western rationalist tradition and the Imami theological tradition. The mediating role is only possible because Munir's Pakistan has exited the Deobandi alignment that made it an extension of the Saudi-Wahhabi axis during the Zia period. The WP-35 (Walayah Pakistan Doctrine) documents the full geopolitical expression of this repositioning.

SCRA Verdict — The Munir Doctrine as Civilizational Completion

The Munir Doctrine is not a new policy departure — it is the completion of the arc that Iqbal charted in the Reconstruction of Religious Thought in Islam (1930) and that Ba'alist Capture interrupted in 1977. The Fitna al-Khawarij designation is the most theologically precise act of any Pakistani head of state since Jinnah's August 11, 1947 address — it deploys a Quranic-Imamic jurisprudential category to definitively distinguish legitimate Islamic authority from its Ba'alist simulacrum. The SCRA reads this as evidence that the Pakistani Army's institutional batin — its self-understanding as the defender of an Iqbalian-Sufi civilizational identity — has survived the Zia glitch and is now operative at the level of state theology.
