I. The Problem with "Civilization"

Say "Islamic Civilization" and the listener imagines: the Golden Age, algebra, the House of Wisdom, minarets, calligraphy, illuminated manuscripts. A historical formation. Something studied in universities. Something that peaked between the 8th and 13th centuries and can be dated, catalogued, and placed in a museum.

This is the wrong register entirely.

The SCRA does not argue about civilizations in the museum sense. The confusion begins with the word "civilization." In this usage, "Islamic civilization" is the civilization of the Golden Age; "Western civilization" is the civilization of the Renaissance and Enlightenment. They are historical objects.
