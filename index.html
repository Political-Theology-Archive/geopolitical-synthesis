Archive Thesis  ·  The Three Fates of the Golden Chain

One synthesis.
Three destinations.

The chain begins with Prophet Idris — identified in Islamic philosophy as Hermes Trismegistus, the pre-Diluvian source of sacred science — from whom two streams descended: the Greek-Hermetic (West) and the Persian-Zoroastrian (East). Both converged at Gondishapur (3rd century CE), the first institutional custodian. Over the next nine centuries, that synthesis traveled three distinct corridors and arrived at three destinations. This archive maps all three.

The Arabic-Islamic synthesis — nine centuries deep, Eastern in origin — crossed into Latin Europe through Toledo. East was the beginning.
